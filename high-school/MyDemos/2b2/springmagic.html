Spring Magic

Wild flowers, love in every heart,
A breeze chases the chill away;
Spring time is for frost to depart,
Ringing in dawn of a brighter day.

The air bears fragrance and chirp,
And the heart is tipped with mirth;
The child enjoys the dollop slurp,
Adults weigh fresh love and worth.

Spring time is the time to declare,
And scream aloud to all in reach;
Your happiness and dreams share,
As you laze on the sprawling beach.

A time for sunshine, time for song,
A time to fall in love with dear life;
It is a time to care, time to throng,
The places that sound jocund fife.

Little compares to nature's beauty,
That fills each blossom and pelagic;
With honeycombs abuzz with duty,
In the dreamy, hearty spring magic.

By Gaynor Borade
